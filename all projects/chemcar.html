Chemically Powered Car

Problem

Some underlying problems with this project are, getting the car to even move in the first place, getting it to move straight, and overcoming air friction.

Solution

Potential solutions for these problems, are making sure the axles aren't to tight in the hole so they can spin allowing the car to move. To make the car move straight the axles must be parallel so we need to have precision so the car won't turn and make sure the axles can't unalign themselves. And for overcoming air friction we need to find a shape for the base that would have the least air resistance.

Ideas

Based on my and other team members research we came up with a few ideas and a basic sketch of how the car could look. The shape with the least resistance is a teardrop shaped body with the round side facing forward. Larger wheels to add some weight to the car so it doesn't spin out when it starts to move and larger wheels take less rotations to go the same distance as smaller wheels. Hollowing out the base and wheels as to not add to much weight since we don't want to use up the chemical reaction getting the car started moving The front axle will have to be longer than the back axle due to the shape of the base.

Assembly

To attach the axels to the car we glued large straw to the bottom of the car then put the axels inside. The wheels we glued on with a form of loctite and the bottle was just glued on since we didn't make a mount of the bottle to sit on.

Expirimentation & testing

Testing the reaction

I wrote down what we did as we tried messing with the reaction, but I had a base measurement of how much of each reactant was needed. For the first time only half of the vinegar was able to fit which was ~45 oz, and we used a lot more baking soda. Since there was so much left the second time we decided to use way less baking soda but we still had some left. The second time we recorded it but or first result was better. The second time had a lot of gas leakage.

Testing the Car

Try #1

The first time we tested our car it didn't move at all and I suspected it was because for the seprator between the vinegar and baking soda I used paper and a lot of the gas escaped through there.

Tweaks

I replaced the paper seperator with a piece of plastic and made sure to glue it well and leave no holes so this time the gas won't leak out through hole and such.
