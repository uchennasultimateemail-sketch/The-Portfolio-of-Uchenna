Freshman Year Inquiry

Research Mapping

Based on my first question I came up with the four following sub topics that I could use to turn into body paragraph. With in these sub topics I came up with more specific things that I could talk about within these categories to create my first map to base my research on.

For my finalized research map I simplified my question so it was more clear and understandable, then I turned the bottom right category and put it into the history one because it was a bit too specific to turn into a paragraph and made more sense as something to mention later.

Completing the Research

The final question I came up with was still poorly phrased, so I changed it again to an even broader topic: "How does Contemporary Art affect the world?" and this is my actual final question that I decided to research on.

Assertions

When I decided on my assertions, two of them were very similar to the ideas I came up with in my research mapping but one of them is different because I was unsure how to get tangible research on it. These where my three main assertions for all of my body paragraphs:

  • It can spark an interest in historical topics and people

  • Give people a deeper understanding of the world and each other

  • Increase emotional intelligence

When I started looking for sources I searched things like "Contemporary Art & ________" the blank being the assertion that I'm searching for evidence for.

Reflection

The most difficult part of the researching process was finding evidence related to my topic. Whenever I searched for Contemporary Art and my assertion, most times out of 10 the results wouldn't only include half of what I needed. It took a lot of digging to find evidence that included both my assertion and its connection to Contemporary Art. I ended up having to find more evidence later because some of my evidence either didn't correlate the way I wanted it or it was really similar to another piece of evidence for the same paragraph.

Research Organizer

This is where I organized my research so I knew where to find my assertion and didn't have to go digging and so I knew what evidence to use for what paragraph. I also summarized what the articles were talking about and how it connected to my overall thesis, the other piece of evidence for that body paragraph, and to know what I was thinking when I picked that evidence.

Research writing

Concerns

One of my main concerns was being able to meet the page criteria of 4-5 pages. Before this I had never written very long informational papers so I was worried that it would fall short. I knew that my analysis would have to be on point and very in-depth because that's what most of the research paper was. Analysis.

Process

When beginning our paper we started by writing out thesis and then our introduction around it. This was by far the easiest part of the essay because it helped put the rest of my essay into perspective. Since my assertions for my body paragraphs changes so much it helped me see what I was writing about. While writing for the body paragraphs I ended up having to do more research because I was unhappy with my evidence or because I needed more to make the point I wanted to make. This made the writing process much longer than it needed to be. Next time I write a paper with evidence I will think about how effective my evidence will be and how I would like it to fit into my paper so I don't have to more more research once that part is over. Shortly before I submitted my paper I had someone read over the entire thing to fix any mistakes that I didn't catch. Once I was finally confident in my work, I submitted.
