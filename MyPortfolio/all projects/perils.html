The Impact of Indifference

It is important to serve your community and not be indifferent to peoples struggles because indifference makes life meaningless and kills humanity. In the speech Perils of Indifference, Wiesel asks "Has the human being become less indifferent and more human?" (Wiesel, 1999, para. 22) This quote shows how Wiesel beleives that indifference makes a human being less human. Because, part of being human is sympathizing with other and indifference is the opposite of that. By being indifferent you not only take your own humaity but also the person you are being indifferent towards by deeming them meaningless for lack of aknowledgemennt. Helping people in your community keeps people from feeling meaningless because their struggles are being recognized. Because, to not be recognized is basically to not exist. In the book Night, Wiesel wrote "For us it meant true equality: nakedness." (Wiesel, 1960, p. 36) This shows the dehumanization that they endured while they were at camps, all forced to be the same and treated the same making all of their lives meaningless. The dehumanization shows how the indifference had taken away their humanity. These two quotes show that the indifference inside a community is bad because it makes the lives of those struggling meaningless and takes away the humanity of everyone involved.

DrawChange

Established in 2009 and headquartered in Atlanta, GA, Drawchange is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated to empowering children from lower socioeconomic situations through the use of art therapy-based programming. Our programs teach coping skills that foster Collaboration, Creation, Dream-building, Empowerment, Imagination, Self-Esteem, and Stress-Relief.

About the founder

As a child, I sought refuge in art. As a first generation American living in the inner city, art was my outlet—a way to cope with life's stresses. My parents immigrated to the United States from Uruguay in the late 70s. As a child of immigrants, I had as much urgency to blend in as to tightly cling to my heritage. It was a challenge to learn to juggle the different cultural pressures. I was forced to assimilate. Art gave me the space and capacity to process and learn to make sense of the dichotomy I was living in. My goal, as founder and CEO of drawchange, is to provide vulnerable children with the same opportunities to be inspired and transformed

Information

Drawchange is located in Atlanta, Orlando, Tuscon, and Puerto Rico as well as internationally in place such as Ethiopia and Costa Rica. Drawchange meets with children in after school programs, orphanages, and homeless shelters


Art Shows

Fostering a sense of community in the children we serve not only aids them in their ability to collaborate with others but also boosts their self-esteem and ability to dream beyond their circumstances. By taking them on field trips such as community-sponsored art shows where they get to proudly display their artwork, group activities such as a visit to a splatter paint studio, or taking them to see others' artwork and creations on display in places they don't normally have access to such as art museums, NASA, and puppet shows, we give them the opportunity to build bonds with each other, their community, and expand their view on what is capable.

Art Camps

Drawchange offers unique and fun summer & school holiday experiences for children aged 5-9 from different socioeconomic backgrounds in Atlanta, GA. Your child will grow confident in their creative expression and in their day-to-day life all while giving back to the community! Reflection

Reading Night by Elie Wiesel and listening to the speech The Perils of Indifference and researching about the organization I found made me realize how important it is to not be indifferent to the issues across the world. Elie says that even anger is better than indifference and I agree because even anger comes to some sort of resolution where no one suffers from indifference but indifference leads to no such thing. In the book you can see how the indifference directly affects him and how it made him feel like the entire world doesn't care about him since its been going on for long. DrawChange isn't indifference to the issue of impoverished children world-wide and decided to do something about it by working on location outside of the US.
