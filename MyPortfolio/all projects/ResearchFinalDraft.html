The Effects of Contemporary Art on the World

Uchenna G. Ikoro

North Gwinnett High School

9th grade literature and composition

Ms. Johnston

23 February 2025

A monochrome painting of  Ultramarine Blue, a musical about a guy you never knew existed till today, and a type of dance that you have never seen before. That is the distinctive yet vast categorization of Contemporary Art, a term used to refer to all art made past the 1970s, not to be confused with Modern art which extends from the 1860s to the 1970s. Contemporary Art is characterized by its ability to communicate ideas and prevalent issues in the world, giving everything a more holistic view, rather than just capturing history like most previous art eras. This current art era does not only refer to painting, but also statues, music, dance, and all the subtopics in between. With such wide horizons, it can spark an interest in historical topics and people, give people a deeper understanding of the world and each other, and increase emotional intelligence. All of these are the positive benefits of Contemporary Art.

History is a hardly mentioned topic. Once students move up a grade or graduate from school, history is easily forgotten and deemed irrelevant or uninteresting compared to things you see on social media by many. Art can spark the excitement of topics deemed useless and boring and make the information more available to the general public who may not want to go digging for historical knowledge on the internet. As stated by Scher, “This Lab project explores contemporary art practitioners’ recent turn to history and the ways in which such new practices developed by visual artists might offer models for, and new forms of dialogue about, the work history does in the world” (Bradley, 2007). This speaks of how new modern practices created by visual art allow for more conversations about what history does for the world. Contemporary Art practices allowing for a new discussion about history means that more and more people will be able to be educated or re-educated on history that was not often talked of before.

Contemporary Art also makes it more interesting to learn in general, seeing a pretty piece on a wall may inspire someone to search out these conversations for himself or herself and want to bring back the important history that is the basis the world runs on. One of the modern practices that has allowed for the reignition of a historical topic was Hamilton: An American Musical made in 2015 in the skilled hands of Lin-Manuel Miranda. He reignited the light of Alexander Hamilton and projected it across the globe (Piner, 2024). And it is not just this musical that reignited a story of history, but many more such as SIX.

Stories from history are also being retold like Homer’s Odyssey through the Epic: The Musical. With more and more musicals coming out about the stories of history, it will not be long before most of history becomes general information because a musical came out about it. This new, catchy way to learn about history makes it not only fun for adults to learn new information, but also for children. Taking your child to watch an appropriate musical whether it be based on history or as accurate as possible, these musicals could carry information that may last them a lifetime.

Contemporary Art being able to reignite stories of history allows for not only a deeper understanding of ourselves and our history but also a deeper understanding of all the people around us and the world as a whole. Connecting with people and really understanding them creates a deeper connection and understanding of the world itself and the bigger picture. Not only that, but also one could make more friends, lower their stress levels, and overall make life more enjoyable. Wesley accounts experiences with Contemporary Art that connected him with other people that he believes he never would have talked to without it. He says, “Through the arts, I’ve worked with people of every conceivable belief system, ability, and socioeconomic background. I’ve laughed, cried, empathized, and connected with people with whom I initially knew of nothing else in common” (Wesley, 2007). This is an example of how art can bring people of different beliefs together. Putting your art out into the world, allows people with similar interests to find and communicate with you and brings companionship as well as conversations. Since art also impacts emotional intelligence, people with differing beliefs can have respectful conversations about what they believe, allowing for a greater understanding of one another. This allows all people, alike or different to have a greater and deeper understanding of each other. But, art's way of connecting us is not only just through relationships. Contemporary Art. As said by Syrotkina, “It participates in the formation of the holistic worldview from the lowest level, which is the feeling and response to the outer world, to the highest, where there is the understanding and acceptance of the world.” (Syrotkina et al., 2022). A holistic worldview is seeing the world as a whole rather than its individual parts. Just because the individual parts are visible does not mean that everyone can put them together to see a whole. Art exercises the ability to be able to put the individual pieces of the world together because it forces people who are open to art to see all the different puzzle pieces and realize that there are so many to put together into the bigger picture. Which is the whole world rather than just the tiny communities that we all live in.

Art is also meant to be interpreted and to induce emotions. This causes people to think about possibly what they feel when they see the painting or how the artist was feeling when he or she made it which exercises emotional intelligence, thereby increasing it. Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability one has to be aware of, control, and express his or her emotions. Such a skill is frequently found in people with leadership positions and is important for maintaining jobs and relationships. According to research done by Travis Bradberry, only 36% of all people have this ability (Cornerstone University, 2021). However, in a study done on children by Da Costa and Keni, a positive relationship between expressive arts and emotional intelligence is shown by higher scores on EQ tests (Da Costa & Keni, 2024). Humans are social creatures who live on connection, but as time moves forward, interactions that exercise our emotional intelligence dwindle causing only so many people to still have high levels of it. Making Contemporary Art more prevalent will raise our emotional intelligence along with our empathy and self-awareness which will raise our emotional intelligence even more, causing a cycle in which the effects are infinite. But, the benefits of art are not limited to children or paintings. In a study done by Joshi, the results showed that the self-esteem and emotional intelligence of the dancers increased with the duration of their practices (Joshi, 2022). Although this study only refers to one type of dance, the benefits span across many types of dance. Dance for many can be a much-needed outlet after a stressful day and a less destructive way to let out harmful emotions that could otherwise be let out on people which can harm the relationships one has made over time. It can also allow for an easier way to portray emotions because, sometimes what you are feeling cannot be put into words. Being able to accurately portray emotions can avoid the frustration of trying to put them into words or even understand them and can allow the self-awareness of pent-up emotions you did not even know you had. This deep understanding of our emotions is the increase in emotional intelligence caused by this art form.

Contemporary Art can reignite the stories of people in history, offer a more interconnected view of the world, and increase emotional intelligence. All of these benefits positively impact the world because, looking around; anyone can see that more of these traits are needed. As mentioned before, only 36% of people have high emotional intelligence, and only so many people knew who Hamilton was before the musical came out, and so many people forget that the world is not just what they see around them but the entire globe. With more of these traits, Contemporary Art improves the world to become a better place where people are understood and connected. Now that the benefits of Contemporary Art are clear, whether it be adding this style of art to teaching or hanging it on more walls, it is important that we try and embed this style of learning, teaching, and sharing information into more daily life so the positive impact grows on the world.
